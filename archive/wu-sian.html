Wu Sian — a teahouse designed to multiply.

Overview

A repeatable space system, built out across 13 stores.

Within 15 years of its short history — starting from Kaohsiung, Taiwan in 2006 — Gong cha now operates 2000+ stores in 25+ countries. The project is named “Wu Sian”, meaning “limitless / infinite” in Chinese, echoing Gong cha’s ambition to become the world’s number-one tea company.

The problem: Gong cha’s interiors lacked consistency in color, layout, materials and furniture — collaborating with multiple interior designers and architects had resulted in confusion, and intricate decorations left customers with an inconsistent perception of the brand. The solution was a unified interior space system: interior planning aligned with brand guidelines, locally sourced materials matched to brand color and material standards, and standardized proportions for brand-color use. The system was built out across 13 store locations in California.

Recognition

Iron A’ Design Award — and seven exhibitions.

The Wu Sian Commercial Teahouse won the Iron A’ Design Award (2021–2022, #137016) in the Interior Space and Exhibition Design category, is featured in the A’ Design Award — Award Winning Interior Design Yearbook 2022 (DesignerPress, Italy, 51/N, ISBN: 978-88-97977-50-6), and was exhibited at Mood, Design Milan at Crystal Hall, the Festival of Architecture and Interior Designing Conference (NSIC and MMRDA grounds), Hebei International Industrial Design Week (Xiongan), the Hebei Design Center Exhibition, and Shenzhen International Industrial Design Week.
